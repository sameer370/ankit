There are several languages in India belonging to different language families.The Constitution of India does not give any language the status of National Language.The official languages of the Union Government of the Republic of India are Hindi in the Devanagari script and English.The Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution lists 22 languages, which have been referred to as scheduled languages and given recognition, status and official encouragement.

According to Census of India of 2001, India has 122 major languages and 1599 other languages.The 2001 Census recorded 30 languages which were spoken by more than a million native speakers and 122 which were spoken by more than 10,000 people.Two contact languages have played an important role in the history of India: Persian and English.Hindi, the most widely spoken language in India today, serves as a lingua franca for much of the country.
